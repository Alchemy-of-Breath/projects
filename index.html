Week 1–2 · Foundation
01 Foundation

Get real about who you are

Before you build anything, get clear. Most breathworkers skip this. Most breathworkers struggle. These are the foundations that hold everything else.

Your niche

Who do you actually want to serve?

  • Burnout recovery, trauma, performance, grief, addiction support — pick one to start
  • The more specific, the more referred. "Breathwork for corporate women in burnout" beats "breathwork for everyone"
  • It can evolve. But you need a stake in the ground now
  • Ask: who do you most want to sit across from in a session?
Your credibility

What you bring beyond the certificate

  • Your personal story is your marketing — be willing to share it truthfully
  • AoB certification is globally recognised and trauma-informed — lead with that
  • Prior professional background (therapist, nurse, coach) is a superpower. Don't hide it
  • You don't need 10 years. You need honesty about where you are
Your minimum setup

What you actually need to start

  • A way to book (Calendly free tier is fine)
  • A way to take payment (Stripe or PayPal)
  • One simple page describing what you do — not a full website
  • A Zoom or a room. That is it. Start before you are "ready"
Honest reality check

Most graduates delay starting because they want everything perfect first. The website, the logo, the brand photography. None of those are what get you clients. Your first ten clients will come from people who already know and trust you. Start there.

The one thing to do today

Write this sentence: "I offer [what] to [who] so they can [result]." That is your positioning. Go back to the business plan and fill it in before you move to the next phase.

Week 2–4 · Offer Design
02 Your Offer

Build your offer stack

Don't start with packages. Start with one thing that works, then build. Here is the architecture that most successful breathwork practices follow.

Free entry point

Lower the threshold to experience the work

  • Free 20-min intro breath session (online)
  • A monthly community breathwork circle (free or donation)
  • Co-host a session on Breathe the World in the AoB community
  • Goal: get people into the breath, not into a sales funnel
1:1 session

Your core income engine to start

  • 75–90 min session: £120–180 (UK), €130–190 (EU), $150–200 (US)
  • Offer a 3 or 6-session container at a slight discount — better outcomes, better income
  • Don't undercharge to feel safe. Undercharging signals low confidence, not generosity
  • Include integration coaching post-session — it is what separates AoB-trained facilitators
Group work

Scale your time without diluting depth

  • Online group session (6–12 people): £35–55/person — highly scalable
  • In-person immersive half-day: £80–120/person
  • Corporate workshop: £500–1500 flat fee — hardest to land, highest return
  • Build group work only after 20+ 1:1 sessions — know what transforms people first
Recurring offer

The stability underneath everything

  • Monthly membership: £30–60/month for 2 group sessions and a library
  • Retainer 1:1: monthly dedicated sessions at a fixed rate
  • Don't launch this until you have social proof and a warm audience
  • 10 members at £40/month = £400 stable base. Build from there

What most graduates get wrong

Creating 5 different packages before they've delivered 5 sessions. Package complexity is a displacement activity. One offer, delivered well, is all you need to build a reputation. Complexity comes later, when you understand what your clients actually need.

Month 1–2 · First Clients
03 First Clients

Get your first 10 clients

These won't come from Instagram. They'll come from your existing life. This phase is about mining what you already have before building what you don't.

The honest sequence

  1. Tell everyone you know.

    Send a personal message (not a broadcast) to 20 people. Tell them what you now do and who you help. Not a pitch — a conversation. Ask if they know anyone who might benefit.

  2. Offer 3 free sessions in exchange for honest testimonials.

    Not discounted — free. Choose people who will give you real feedback and write real words about the experience. Testimonials are your first marketing asset.

  3. Partner with one complementary practitioner.

    A yoga teacher, therapist, nutritionist, or coach. Refer to each other. One trusted referral partner is worth more than a thousand Instagram followers.

  4. Host one free community session.

    Online or in person. Invite your network. At the end, make one offer. The 60-second bridge close from experience to invitation is one of the highest-converting moments in this work.

  5. Show up consistently in the AoB community.

    Alumni are your warmest potential referrers. Mighty Networks is already populated with people who understand the depth of AoB work. Be present, contribute, ask for referrals directly.

  6. Don't wait for the website.

    Most first clients book from a conversation, not a page. The website is for credibility when they Google you after a recommendation. Build that relationship first.

The thing nobody says out loud

Selling breathwork is uncomfortable for most graduates because the work feels sacred and selling feels commercial. The reframe: you are not selling. You are making the work accessible to people who need it. Charging fairly is an act of integrity, not exploitation. If you undercharge, you signal that the work isn't worth much. It is.

Month 2–6 · Marketing
04 Marketing

Content that converts

Marketing breathwork requires education before invitation. People can't buy something they don't yet understand the value of. Your job is to close the knowledge gap.

Instagram and Facebook

Where your audience already is

  • 3 posts/week minimum. Below that, the algorithm forgets you exist
  • Pillar split: 60% education (nervous system, the science, the why), 40% personal and invitation
  • Reels of live breath moments outperform static posts 4:1 — show the work, not just words about it
  • Caption formula: hook (1–2 lines) then truth (2–3 sentences) then one soft invitation
  • Hashtags: #breathwork #nervoussystemregulation #traumainformed — specific beats broad
Email list

The one asset you actually own

  • Start an email list from day one. 50 subscribers who trust you beats 5,000 followers who don't
  • A weekly or fortnightly short letter — not a newsletter. Personal, real, one insight
  • Lead magnet: a short guided breath practice (audio) or "5 signs your nervous system needs support" PDF
  • ConvertKit free tier (up to 1,000 subscribers) is enough to start
Podcasts and guesting

Borrowed audiences that convert

  • Guest on 2–3 podcasts in wellness, mental health, or coaching spaces
  • Pitch: "I can speak to the neuroscience behind breathwork and run a live practice with your audience"
  • One good podcast appearance brings 5–15 warm enquiries
  • Don't start your own podcast until you have consistent clients and content
In-person presence

The highest conversion channel

  • Wellness events, yoga studios, therapy CPD events — attend and offer to co-facilitate or speak
  • Corporate wellbeing days — HR teams actively seek evidence-based practices
  • One free talk with a 20-min breath experience at a local wellness space converts at 20–40%
  • The breath speaks for itself when people experience it. Get them into a session
Marketing myth to drop now

More content does not equal more clients. Consistent, specific, honest content that meets the nervous system where it is — that's what converts. One piece of content that lands deeply is worth fifty generic posts that don't.

The AoB Caption Formula (use this)

1
Hook
1–2 lines. Evocative or surprising. Never start with "I".
  • "Most people breathe 18,000 times a day without thinking about it."
  • "Your nervous system is listening to every single one."
  • "She came not knowing what she needed. She left knowing who she was."
2
Truth
2–3 sentences grounded in science or story.
  • Name the mechanism (nervous system, vagus nerve, CO2 tolerance)
  • Connect it to a feeling or lived experience the reader will recognise
  • Don't over-explain. One clean insight is enough
3
Invitation
One sentence. Soft and specific. Never pushy.
  • "If you feel the calling, there is a link in bio."
  • "Book a free 30-minute consultation — no pressure, just clarity."
  • "Follow for more on the science and the soul of the breath."

The income ladder — what to expect

  • Stage 1months 1–3£0–500/month

  • Stage 2months 3–6£500–1,500/month
    8–12 regular clients. Group sessions beginning. Social proof building.
  • Stage 3months 6–12£1,500–3,000/month
    Referral engine running. First membership income. Possibly first corporate work.
  • Stage 4year 2+£3,000–6,000+/month
    Facilitator with established reputation. Mix of 1:1, group, retreats, corporate. This is where the AoB community's ongoing training investment starts to compound significantly.
The income truth most training companies won't tell you

Building a breathwork practice to full-time income takes 12–18 months. Keep a part-time income source for the first year if you can. It removes the desperation that clients can feel and gives you the space to build slowly and well. The practices that last are not the ones built on urgency.

Ongoing · Sustainability
06 Sustain

The practitioner who lasts

Most burnout in this field isn't about overwork. It's about under-regulation. Here is what keeps you in the work for decades, not just years.

Your own practice

Non-negotiable, not aspirational

  • Daily or near-daily personal breathwork practice is the foundation of everything
  • You cannot hold a nervous system you haven't learned to hold yourself
  • The I Am Alchemy Community on Mighty Networks gives you access to a practice library — use it
  • Regular 1:1 sessions with a more experienced facilitator — budget for this as a business expense
Continued learning

The lineage keeps giving

  • Attend Breath Camp — it deepens your practice and reconnects you to the AoB circle
  • Pursue CPD in trauma, somatic work, or a specialist modality relevant to your niche
  • VIP Membership gives monthly access to Anthony and Amy — rare in any training lineage
  • The most effective marketing in this field is being visibly in your own ongoing development
Community

You are not meant to do this alone

  • Stay connected to the AoB alumni network — peer supervision, referrals, co-facilitation
  • Join or create a peer supervision group with other graduates (monthly Zoom)
  • Celebrate other facilitators publicly — generosity in this field compounds in unexpected ways
  • The AoB circle is a genuine competitive advantage. Most breathworkers don't have it
Review rhythm

What gets measured, improves

  • Monthly: client numbers, income, enquiry source — where are your leads actually coming from?
  • Quarterly: revisit your niche, offer, and pricing — one of these usually needs a small adjustment
  • Every 6 months: ask your best clients what brought them and what they'd tell a friend
  • Annual: do you still love this work? If the answer is complicated, investigate — don't suppress it
The long game

The breathworkers still practising and thriving in 10 years are not the ones who hustled hardest in year one. They are the ones who stayed regulated, stayed curious, and stayed connected to why they started. The work will sustain you if you let it. Let it.
